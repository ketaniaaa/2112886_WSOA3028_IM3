Design Guide

Aura Trend

Ive noticed a trend online that uses radial gradients to describe auras. They are used often as poster design and are now seeing a web design trend. Given Cora's personality and status as a medium, I thought that she would be inclined to like this design trend. I have implemented radial gradients into the design on the website. Although this trend usually consists of many pastel tones, I stuck to the same shade of orange that I have used througout the site for uniformity. I also noticed that these images of auras usually include outlined shapes of stars, moons and orbs and bold oversized text. I have implemented this into my site as I made decorative images to be placed amongst the character content to reflect this trend.

This trend's use of shape also reflects Cora's personality as the use of stars and moons allude to esoteric symbolism. The use of these symbols correlates directly to Cora and helps with the visual understanding of the site so that users are aware they are browsing through a spiritual website.

Brutalism

The main inspiration for the layout of my website was Brutalism. I spent a lot of time on Behance for inspiration. Often times, layouts with alot of white space and solid borders caught my attention. I did more research about the design movement and thought it aligned with Cora. The minimalist of this design movement allowed for the implementation of the esoteric elements without overloading the user. Brutalism makes use of strong structural elements, like the architeture movement of the same name. I have done this by including thick borders. The architectural style also makes use of line to create harsh shadows. I used this as inspiration in my web design by making hard box borders a feature design element. Brutalism also tends to prefer muted and minimal color schemes. My website mainly deploys only three colors and images are often filtered to be greyscaled in order to fit a minimal look. The architeture style also emphasizes the concrete of buildings. I adapted this by implementing a transparent textured background. I used an image of stars instead of concrete due to Cora's personality.

I also chose Brutalism because of its use of white space. White space in web design allows for users to avoid being overwhelmed by visual elements. This seemed like it would be a goal of Cora's. As a spiritualist, she works with creating peace within her clients and therefore would want a clam online space for her users to explore.

Colour Scheme

I have chosen a minimal colour palette in order to communicate a minimal design. I liked the idea of a black and white website with a pop of colour. When approaching the research for my site, I came across swiss web design. I noticed that a lot of jewel toned colors are used alongside otherwise monochrome schemes. I chose this orange because it has been featured in swiss design but also because the pairing of orange and black tends to remind people of halloween. This connatation of the color scheme was aligned with Cora's image as a person and her interests.

Font

I have chosen to use Space Mono and Work Sans because they are minimal. Space Mono, I noticed, was often used in print design that made use of oversized text. I liked the contrast of the harsh font that looks like an early web font against the more trendy minimalism of the other elements of the cite. I used Work Sans for the main sections of the articles for easier readability.

Logo

As cora is a technomancer, I took this into consideration when creating the logo for the site. I made this made the logo in Illustator myself. The logo consists of a globe that has a mesh on it to relicate the www logo to indicate the internet. This globe is placed on a based to look like a crystal ball in order to communicate that the character is a witch and that users entering the site are entering an esoteric site. This is further supported by the inclusion of other esoteric symbolism such as stars. I made variations of the logo for both the title icon and the header. I knew I wanted my header to consist of black copy and therefore in order to logo to appear coherent with the navigation. The title icon was made white and thicker for visability. The icon is used in order to allow the user to know what site each tab in their brower indicates.

Responsivity

The design choiced I made for the site to be responsive and accessibile mainly pertain to the navigation of the site. In order to create a more responsive site, I ensure that there was a collapsable menu. I indicated the presences of an expandable menu by using a hamburger menu when the screen size is changed.

I made sure the Colour palette is simple and high contrast. The use of high contrast is known to be more accessible to visually impaired users. This is prefered to color palette with little variation as contrasting colors are easier to differentiate. In regards to my visual cues, I ensured that my navigational links had a hover effect in order to communicate an interactive aspect of the site to the user. I did this by changeing the background colors on my header navigation and creating coloured outlines in my footer links.

I also made most of my sizing decisons based on responsivity. For most of my boxes, when looking at my CSS, i have used 1em padding and borders in order to keep my formats uniform. I wanted my content to also make use of white space within the boxes they are situated. Sizing of images and widths are controlled using percentages instead of fixed units. The only instance of using pixels was in regard to my border decorations.
