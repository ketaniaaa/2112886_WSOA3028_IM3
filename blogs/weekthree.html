This week we will be discussing ‘Medium is the Message’. I believe that this should be a required reading for anyone that is a producer of content that will be received by an audience. McLuhan’s work has reshaped the way that communication is perceived and constructed.

Marshal McLuhan is notorious for his work “Medium is the Message”. Usually, the contents of communication are highlighted rather than the medium in which the content is presented. McLuhan proposed a new focus on the form that the message takes. He says that the form of the content is equally as important as the content itself. This is because forms or mediums have the ability to create large social impacts and sway society. He further goes on to say that if one does not fundamentally understand the medium, then they do not fully understand the message.

McLuhan is adamant that mediums are not neutral. This is due to their ability to influence the environments around it. Let us look at the shift from oral to print media. The use of oral storytelling had implications on the story being told. This can be in stories being altered when retold due to human error and a lack of documentation, thus rendering the information being spoken less likely to survive history. Print media allowed for the message to be better documented and received. The rise of print media also encouraged higher rates of literacy and enabled a shift to humans preferring more visual communication. This demonstrates how the medium of which content is packaged has a direct influence on its recipients. We can see that in order to best distribute a message, the medium must be carefully considered due to our social preferences.

The age of the internet has further transformed the conventions of communication and provided an array of new mediums in which to present information. I think that is important to note that the internet is not one medium itself but also contains various mediums through its support for a variety of media forms. The internet has allowed for quick communication that is not hindered by distance. The introduction of the web as a new medium has had an influence of previous mediums of communication, such as written word. Society has become accustomed to using the web as a main form of communication. The internet has allowed for an even greater shift towards a more visual language. It is noted that web users are less likely to engage with long blocks of texts due to their familiarity of the visual aspects that the web offers. This directly influences how the web is designed as white space and aesthetic components have to be considered for the message to be best received. I say that the web also supports a variety of mediums as one can access video, audio, imagery and written content. While doing research for this blog post, I found that I prefer to watch videos with strong infographics rather than long essays. The rise of YouTube and po of podcasts suggests that the web is reviving human’s inclination to audio communication. This shows how the internet has transformed the manner in which messages have to be constructed.
