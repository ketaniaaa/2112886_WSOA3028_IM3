Design is said to shape the world around it. The ability to influence an environment makes designers responsible for the design they create. This week we will take a look at the ethics of user experience design and why it is so important.

UX is fundamentally linked to ethics as ethics serve to situate our conduct and understanding of the world and user experience designers require insight on user’s behaviour. Ultimately, designers are reliant on understanding their user’s ethics and must adhere to guidelines. Technology has largely become imbedded into everyday life and every interaction with the internet requires mediation of how your experience of that area of the web will be experienced. User experience design is required everywhere and therefore must be done right! Ethical user experience is also fundamental as UX is a commercialized field. Being commercial requires ethics to be adhered to when dealing wit consumers.

User experience seeks to satisfy human needs. This human aspect of the tech industry places emphasis on emotion and experience of technology derived from human morals and ethics. This means that designers have the responsibly of retaining empathy for their users. Users are NOT the computers that we use to create the code that facilitates their experiences, but complex ranges on individuals. The designer takes on the responsibility of catering for users in a morally good way by centring design around users’ needs and their wanted experience. When designing an experience, it is required to access the behavioural patterns of the user. This raises the need for rational discourse. The facilitation for user behaviour thus requires the acknowledgement of ethical practice.

The HCI guidelines aim to uphold design ethics. This provides the rationality to understanding the impact of designed experiences The guidelines provide a framework for which designers are responsible and aware of their moral impact their design choices. The HCI allows for both the ethics of users and designers to be protected and therefore creates a standard for ethical design practice. This allows for a better and clearer understanding our ethical user experience design.

Other examples of ethical user experience include transparency between the user experience designer and the user. When creating UX, research is a fundamental part of the design process. This allows for designers to receive necessary information regarding their user’s behaviour. Research must be upheld to a standard that allows for a lack of bias and protects users with the knowledge that their behaviour is not being immorally observed by user experience designers.

Ethics is crucial when working with people. The experience that is created has impact on the daily lives of many and therefore should be designed responsibly. This article has demonstrated that the road to more ethical user experience hinges on understanding of users as people. Empathy for our users allows for better and morally sound design!
